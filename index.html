Bio

I am a computational scientist working hard to positively impact human health. I am unusually interdisciplinary, having developed deep expertise in biology, functional genomics, human genetics, and machine learning. I am most effective when combining these.

In the past:

  • I have led a machine learning team at Altos Labs in its Institute of Computation, developing large language models for epigenetics.
  • I identified novel targets for therapeutic intervention in SLE at Genomics, and developed complete therapeutic stories for them through the combination of my skills.
    • I have returned for a short stint at Genomics to build their gene expression platform in Mystra, and prototype AI agent-based access and analysis of their databases.
  • I pioneered the first analyses of large-scale single-cell RNA-sequencing data during my PhD in John Marioni’s lab.

Education

  • PhD - Marioni Lab, University of Cambridge, 2015-19
  • BSc, Natural Sciences - University College London, 2012-15
